Phases 1-2: Discover & Define (Weeks 1-2)

The first two weeks were dedicated to empathy and synthesis. Teams immersed themselves in the problem by mapping the human ecosystem and patient journey, then synthesized this research into a clear Point of View (POV) statement and a wide array of "How Might We..." questions to guide ideation.

Week 1 Deliverables

  • User Profiles (Maria & Shawna)
  • Patient Journey Map (Start)

Week 2 Deliverables

  • Refined POV Statement
  • 20-30 "How Might We..." Questions

'How Might We' Goal

This chart shows the target range (20-30) for HMW questions, a key metric for successful divergent thinking in this phase.

Phase 3: Ideate (Week 3)

With a clear problem defined, Week 3 focused on divergent ideation. Facilitated sessions used techniques like "Brainstorming by Sense" and "Dot Voting" to generate a wide range of creative solutions, ultimately converging on the "Calming Kit Bundle" concept and storyboarding its use.

Key Deliverables

  • "Calming Kit Bundle" Concept
  • 6-Panel Storyboard (Nurse/Shawna Workflow)

Brainstorm by Sense

This donut chart provides a hypothetical breakdown of ideas generated during the "Brainstorm by Sense" activity, showing how constraining the brainstorm can lead to varied ideas across different sensory modalities.

Phase 4: Prototype & Test (Week 4)

Week 4 was about making ideas tangible. Teams built low-fidelity physical prototypes of their calming kits and cart models. They also used "Service Prototypes" (role-playing) to test the workflow, gathering structured feedback using the "I Like / I Wish / What If" matrix.

Key Activities & Deliverables

  • Maker Session & Low-Fi Prototyping
  • Service Prototype (Role-Playing)
  • Peer-to-Peer Testing
  • Gathered "I Like / I Wish / What If" Feedback

Prototype Feedback (Example)

A radar chart shows hypothetical feedback scores for the prototype. This method helps visualize strengths and weaknesses across key criteria like feasibility, desirability, and user impact.

Phase 5: Finalize & Present (Week 6)

After a week of iteration (Week 5), the final week was dedicated to refinement and presentation. Teams conducted a final dress rehearsal and prepared their 3-minute pitch decks, culminating in the "Pitch-A-Thon" where they presented their finalized prototypes and solutions.

Final Deliverables

  • Finalized Physical Prototype
  • Finalized 3-Minute Pitch Deck
  • 3-Minute Pitch Presentation
