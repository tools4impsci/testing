The iSense Project Timeline

IDT x BCSSW

This project visualizes the six-week process for undergraduate teams tackling the "iSense challenge." The workflow strictly follows the five phases of Design Thinking, progressing from deep user understanding to tangible prototyping and a final presentation.

Project Phases & Duration (Gantt Chart)

The core project timeline is organized by Design Thinking phases, showing exact week numbers and the associated dates for each activity. The timeline progresses chronologically from bottom to top.

Phase 1 & 2: Discover & Define (Wks 1-2)

The first two weeks (Oct 30 - Nov 6) are crucial for Empathy and Synthesis. Teams immerse themselves in the problem space, mapping the human ecosystem and patient journey, and finalizing a clear Point of View (POV) statement and 20-30 "How Might We..." questions for the next phase.

Key Activities

  • Mapping the Human Ecosystem workshop
  • Patient Journey Mapping
  • Refined POV Statement development

Phase 3: Ideate (Wk 3)

Week 3 (Nov 13) focuses on Divergent Ideation. Teams use techniques like "Brainstorming by Sense" and SCAMPER to generate a high quantity of creative solutions. The week concludes with Dot Voting to converge on the final "Calming Kit Bundle" concept and storyboard its use.

Key Deliverables

  • Concept Convergence
  • 6-Panel Storyboard

Phase 4: Prototype & Test (Wk 4)

In Week 4 (Nov 20), ideas become tangible. Teams conduct a Maker Session for low-fidelity physical prototypes, and perform a Service Prototype (role-playing) to test the user experience. Structured feedback is gathered using the "I Like / I Wish / What If" matrix.

Key Activities

  • Low-Fidelity Prototyping
  • Peer-to-Peer Testing
  • Feedback Matrix Analysis

Phase 5: Iteration & Final Pitch (Wks 5-6)

Week 5 (Nov 21 - Dec 1) is an implicit Iteration and Refinement period where teams apply feedback. Week 6 (Dec 2 - Dec 4) is dedicated to the final presentation, including a dress rehearsal, preparation of the 3-minute pitch deck, and culminating in the "Pitch-A-Thon" for the final delivery.

Final Deliverables (Week 6)

  • Finalized Physical Prototype
  • 3-Minute Pitch Presentation
