The iSense Project Timeline

A 6-Week Design Thinking Journey

This project visualizes the six-week process for undergraduate teams tackling the "iSense challenge." The workflow strictly follows the five phases of Design Thinking, progressing from deep user understanding to tangible prototyping and a final presentation.

Phases & Duration (Gantt Overview)

The core project timeline is organized by Design Thinking phases, showing how time investment shifts from foundational research to refinement and final delivery.

Phase 1 & 2: Discover & Define (Weeks 1-2)

The first two weeks are crucial for **Empathy and Synthesis**. Teams immerse themselves in the problem space, mapping the human ecosystem and patient journey, and finalizing a clear Point of View (POV) statement and 20-30 "How Might We..." questions for the next phase.

Key Activities

  • Mapping the Human Ecosystem workshop
  • Patient Journey Mapping
  • Refined POV Statement development

Phase 3: Ideate (Week 3)

Week 3 focuses on **Divergent Ideation**. Teams use techniques like "Brainstorming by Sense" and **SCAMPER** to generate a high quantity of creative solutions. The week concludes with **Dot Voting** to converge on the final "Calming Kit Bundle" concept and storyboard its use.

Key Deliverables

  • Concept Convergence
  • 6-Panel Storyboard

Phase 4: Prototype & Test (Week 4)

In Week 4, ideas become tangible. Teams conduct a **Maker Session** for low-fidelity physical prototypes, and perform a **Service Prototype** (role-playing) to test the user experience. Structured feedback is gathered using the "I Like / I Wish / What If" matrix.

Key Activities

  • Low-Fidelity Prototyping
  • Peer-to-Peer Testing
  • Feedback Matrix Analysis

Phase 5: Iteration & Final Pitch (Weeks 5-6)

Week 5 is an implicit **Iteration and Refinement** period where teams apply feedback. Week 6 is dedicated to the final presentation, including a dress rehearsal, preparation of the 3-minute pitch deck, and culminating in the "Pitch-A-Thon" for the final delivery.

Final Deliverables (Week 6)

  • Finalized Physical Prototype
  • 3-Minute Pitch Presentation
